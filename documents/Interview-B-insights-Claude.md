# Podcast Discovery Research Insights - Participant 2

## Research Question 1: Current Podcast Listening Behaviors

### Triggers (what reminds them to listen)
- **Platform notifications**: Relies on Spotify to show new episodes (blue dot indicator)
- **Activity-based**: Specific activities trigger podcast listening (cooking, running, driving, walking)
- **Mood-dependent**: Chooses based on whether wanting to learn or be distracted
- **Guest/interviewee-based**: Sees a celebrity/soccer player interview and tries that podcast

### Context (how, where, when, how often)

#### Frequency
- **Multiple times a week** to daily
- Varies based on schedule and activities

#### When/Where
- **Cooking** (often, props device on microwave)
- **Over lunch** (when can get a lunch break)
- **Running** (full episodes)
- **Walking**
- **Long drives** (queues up multiple episodes, only when alone in car)
- **Before bed/napping** (mentioned as potential use case)
- **Not while driving in the city** (needs podcasts that don't require full attention)

#### How Long
- **Full episodes** when running or on long drives
- **~20 minutes** while cooking or eating lunch
- Often doesn't finish episodes - "I'll either come back to it or I'll forget to come back to it and then that one's just kind of gone"

### Content (show types, lengths, appeal, management)

#### Show Types (Current Favorites)
1. **Heavyweight** - favorite podcast, serialized storytelling wrapped in single episodes
2. **Serial** - what got them into podcasts, serialized format
3. **Legal deep-dive podcasts** about Serial case
4. **Two Dykes and a Mic** - comedy, doesn't require full attention
5. **The Moth** - though stories not long enough
6. **Women's soccer podcasts**
7. **Queer interview podcasts** ("interesting interviews with weird people")
8. **Call Her Daddy** - only for specific interviewees
9. **Search Engine** - techie, answering interesting questions
10. **This American Life** - used to love, now burnt out
11. **The Daily** - used to listen, now burnt out (too much news)
12. **Domestic violence/legal case podcasts** related to work interests

#### Appeal Factors (CRITICAL - Very Specific Criteria)

**Primary Appeal: "Good Storytelling"**
- **Long enough to be invested** - The Moth stories too short
- **Thoughtful and in-depth**
- Makes complex topics accessible with enough hook
- Sees things from different sides, multiple perspectives
- **Intentional segmentation** - each episode has own theme but builds into larger story
- Different from TV - "each one has to kind of have its own theme, but also like build in some way into the story"

**Production Quality Requirements:**
- **High production value** - "pretty well thought out"
- **Good sound quality**
- **Well edited**
- **Structured** - keeps things moving along
- **Intentional and well planned** - can tell within "a few minutes" if it's working
- Avoids rambling or going off topic

**Host Qualities:**
- Good interviewer (keeps things moving, asks good questions)
- Example: "Alex Cooper... is a good interviewer, she does keep things moving along"

**Attention Requirements:**
- **Filters by attention level needed** based on activity
- Heavyweight requires full attention (like an audiobook)
- Two Dykes and a Mic can be background while driving in city

**Topics of Interest:**
- Critical examinations of true crime (not sensationalized)
- Legal cases with nuance
- Women's soccer
- Queer perspectives
- Tech topics
- Issues related to their work (domestic violence law, criminal justice)

#### Journalism Background Influence
- Has journalism experience
- **Podcasts have replaced long-form journalism reading**
- "Attention span to read long-form journalism has diminished, unfortunately"
- Appreciates same narrative arc as written journalism
- Recognizes overlap between good podcasting and good journalism

#### Management for Finding New Shows
- **Relies heavily on Spotify's interface** - "not a sophisticated Spotify user"
- Depends on platform to show which podcasts they listen to
- Loses track of podcasts easily
- **Wishes for rating/prioritization system** - frustrated that Call Her Daddy appears in top 8 when they rarely listen
- **Does NOT actively manage** - knows features exist (saving, starring) but never uses them
- "I do a really bad job of it"
- Same pattern with TV shows - makes mental notes, forgets

## Research Question 2: How Do They Discover New Shows?

### Primary Discovery Methods

#### 1. Personal Recommendations (Preferred but underutilized)
- **Friends and romantic partner** - "when Shelby and I first started dating, we sent each other podcast episodes"
- **Siblings**
- **Recognizes this is best method** - "that's the best way to get to one that I would actually like"
- **Aspirational** - "I should ask friends more"

#### 2. Platform Recommendations (Spotify)
- "You might like this podcast"
- Cross-recommendations based on listening
- **Often disappointing** - doesn't trust Spotify to find what they want

#### 3. Guest/Celebrity Appearances
- Sees a soccer player or celebrity being interviewed
- Tries that podcast, then tries more episodes

#### 4. Articles/Media Coverage
- Reads an article about a podcast online
- Finds and marks it on Spotify

#### 5. Cross-Pollination
- Heavyweight does crossover with Search Engine

### Discovery Challenges
- **Can't search for specific formats** on Spotify (e.g., "serial podcasts")
- Would have to Google "best serial podcasts" externally
- **Lack of sophisticated sorting/filtering** in current platform

### Burn Out Pattern (IMPORTANT)
> "I burn out of them pretty quickly"

- Gets fixated on a podcast
- Listens to ton of episodes
- Within a few weeks: "I'm not going to listen to that podcast again"
- Or just forgets and moves on

**Examples:**
- This American Life - used to love, now has trouble getting through episodes
- The Daily - used to listen all the time, burnt out
- Even starting to lose patience with Heavyweight

**Why Burn Out Happens:**
- "Tone and arc... the people are too familiar"
- Gets annoying when you know exactly how storytelling will go
- "This American Life has such a clear way of storytelling and Ira Glass is such a distinctive [voice]"
- **Element of surprise is important** - new voices, new storytelling approaches

## Research Question 3: How Do They Decide to Continue Listening?

### Trial Period
- **"Within a few minutes, I can tell whether that's working for me or not"**
- Very quick assessment
- For new podcasts, doesn't start with most recent - **finds episode with most interesting guest or topic**

### Decision Criteria (In Order of Importance)

#### 1. Intentional and Well Thought Out (PRIMARY)
**Must have:**
- Structure
- Good pacing
- Keeps things moving along (especially for interviews)

#### 2. Production Quality (CRITICAL)
- High production value
- Good sound quality
- Well edited
- Professional execution

**Quote:**
> "If I'm going to listen to one and that's going to be my main activity while I'm driving or running or whatever, I would rather listen to one that's pretty well thought out"

#### 3. Engaging Storytelling
- Not rambling
- Not going off topic
- Has an "interesting story"
- Accessible but with depth

#### 4. Host Quality
- Good interviewer skills
- Asks good questions
- Stays on topic

### Deal-Breakers
- **Low production quality**
- **Rambling or lack of structure**
- **"Lesser known ones that just pop up somewhere"** - experiments with these but production quality usually disappoints
- **Too much familiarity** - can identify storytelling formula too easily, loses patience

## Key Themes & Patterns

### 1. High Standards for Production Quality
- **Very discerning listener** with journalism background
- Can tell within minutes if podcast meets standards
- Production quality often trumps topic interest
- "If I'm going to listen... I would rather listen to one that's pretty well thought out"

### 2. Burn Out and Familiarity Fatigue
- **Pattern of intense engagement → rapid burn out**
- Gets too familiar with host's voice, storytelling patterns
- **Element of surprise is crucial**
- Even favorites (Heavyweight) starting to wear thin
- This American Life and The Daily - former favorites, now burnt out

### 3. Platform Dependency and Frustration
- Heavily reliant on Spotify but frustrated with it
- "Not a sophisticated Spotify user"
- Doesn't trust platform recommendations
- **Wishes for better management tools** but doesn't use existing ones
- Call Her Daddy appearing in top 8 frustrates them

### 4. Aspiration vs. Reality Gap
- **Knows friends are best source** - "that's the best way" - but doesn't ask enough
- **Knows management tools exist** but doesn't use them
- "I should" do things differently
- Makes mental notes about shows, forgets
- **Would like better tools but admits might not use them**

### 5. Activity-Context Matching
- **Carefully matches podcast to activity** based on attention requirements
- Heavyweight = full attention (like audiobook)
- Two Dykes and a Mic = can be distracted
- Different needs for running vs. cooking vs. driving in city

### 6. Work-Life Balance in Content
- Work involves bipartisan politics and news
- **Wants escape from daily news cycle** in personal podcast time
- Burnt out on The Daily because "too much daily news"
- Doesn't want opposing viewpoints - gets that at work
- But does want deeper contextual dives into issues (just not daily news)

### 7. Journalism-Influenced Listening
- **Podcasts have replaced long-form journalism reading**
- Appreciates same qualities: multiple perspectives, accessibility, intentional structure
- Serial got them into podcasts (investigative journalism style)
- But attention span for reading has diminished

### 8. Practicality Over Novelty
- **App wins over physical devices** - most practical
- Handheld wins over desk - more functional
- Doesn't want to purchase new device
- Doesn't want to carry extra device with phone
- Would use physical device only in specific, limited contexts

## Recommendations

### Highest Priority

1. **Build the app (Concept 11) as primary product**
   - This participant's clear preference
   - Addresses frustration with Spotify
   - No adoption barrier (new device purchase)
   - Most practical for their multi-context use

2. **Include format filtering (Concept 3)**
   - Critical gap in current platforms
   - Can't search for "serial podcasts" anywhere
   - Would use this feature

3. **Breaking news vs. evergreen distinction (Concept 4)**
   - Addresses burnout with daily news
   - Want deeper dives with context
   - Separate from "news in your feed" format

4. **Quality signals/filtering**
   - This participant judges quality in minutes
   - Help them identify high-production shows quickly
   - Production value indicators
   - User ratings/reviews from people with similar standards

### Medium Priority

5. **Tone-based grouping with multiple tags (Concept 1)**
   - Mood-based listening is real
   - Must allow multiple tags per show
   - Prevent tag inflation

6. **Time-of-day surfacing (Concept 8) - IF customizable**
   - Must let users set their own preferences
   - Default assumptions won't work
   - Could be powerful if personalized

7. **Better episode selection for new podcasts**
   - Don't auto-play most recent
   - For serialized: start at Episode 1
   - For episodic: surface most popular/highly-rated episode
   - This participant specifically seeks "most interesting" episode when trying new show

8. **Friend recommendation features**
   - They recognize friends are best source
   - Don't use it enough because it requires active asking
   - Make it easier: "What are your friends listening to?"
   - Social sharing of specific episodes

### Lower Priority

9. **Local events (Concept 2) - Only with quality filters**
   - Skepticism about production quality
   - Would need to guarantee high-quality, well-produced content
   - Otherwise topic relevance doesn't matter

10. **Cross-referencing (Concept 6)**
    - "Not top of list"
    - Keeps you in same genre
    - Useful but not differentiating

### Avoid

11. **Opposing viewpoints (Concept 7)**
    - Not what people want from leisure listening
    - If work already requires balanced perspectives, podcast = escape

12. **Physical devices as primary product**
    - Nice-to-have but not practical enough
    - Won't displace phone-based listening
    - Limited use cases
    - Barrier of purchasing new device

### Design Principles from This Participant

**1. Respect High Standards**
- Don't surface low-quality podcasts just because they match category
- Production quality signals matter
- User can tell in minutes - help them filter quickly

**2. Address Familiarity Fatigue**
- Help users discover new voices/approaches
- Don't just recommend similar shows
- Promote variety in storytelling styles
- Track when user might be burning out (declining engagement)

**3. Bridge Aspiration-Reality Gap**
- Participant wants to manage better but doesn't
- Auto-manage for them based on behavior
- Make good defaults
- Don't require active curation

**4. Match Content to Context**
- Surface right type of podcast for activity
- Learn attention-level preferences
- Heavyweight-type shows for focused listening
- Comedy for distracted listening

**5. Quality Over Quantity**
- Better to show fewer high-quality options
- Analysis paralysis is real
- Removing photos/descriptions helps decision-making
- "Minimize options"
