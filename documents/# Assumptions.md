# Assumptions

## tflo

Market/product fit
1. (If we pursue this beyond a hobby project) People are used to spending very little for consummer goods, we can't compete on price alone, we'll need something that is hard to replicate

Technical
1. There may be a reason why this doesn't exist already, we just don't know it yet. My assumption is that it's technical or something to do with API limitations.

Process
1. Greg will lead software, I'll lead research and design, but we'll both make product decisions and split admin work
2. If we find traction, we'll want to pursue a commercial sale of it
3. Turning a hobby project into a consummer good will involve learning new processes, such as CE certification, manufacturing, and finding capital

UX/design
1. The idea of browsing radio stations *may* not translate well for podcasts, this may be because of the podcast format or existing interactions/expecations of podcasts
2. The beginning of a podcast episode may be the least exciting way to tease it when browsing "station to station"
3. The radio will have minimal digital interfaces and interactions, it will be mostly analog buttons and dials
4. The radio will be accessible to someone with limited dexterity, as much as we can make it
5. The interaction and mental model *might* be the hardest or the easiest question to solve
6. We don't want to use AI but we might end up exploring its use at some point (e.g. summaries)
7. We'll want a modern take on the design vs as a vintage radio

## gmusick

Assumptions

Device
- We are building a physical device.
- That device plays podcasts and only podcasts.  No radio, no mp3s, no streaming music services.
- This will be a premium product.
- The device is small enough to sit on a table or nightstand, but does not fit in your pocket. You have to plug it in. It's not meant to be portable.
- The primary interface is on the device itself. It doesn't need a companion app or anything to work.
- The device will contain a speaker.
- You can connect headphones to it (likely bluetooth).
- The device will have a retro look and feel.

Project
- On a scale of "for fun" to "profit", this will be on the "for fun" side.
- It will be sold to the public.
- In addition to our time, we will have to spend our own money on this.

Process
- This is a side gig for us.
- Therefore, to make any progress, we'll have to push each other to deliver.
- This is a two-person team.
- No silos (e.g. one person does software, the other does hardware).
- We will meet at weekly (at least).
