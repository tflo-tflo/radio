# Research brief: Interviews

## Background
Today, people continue to use manual or passive methods to find new podcast shows (e.g. word-of-mouth, "trending" podcast lists, etc.)

## Research goals
1. Better understand current behavior (triangulate with secondary research findings)
2. Test early concepts that include different formats (i.e. a physical device)

## Research questions
1. What are their current podcast listening behaviors?
   1. Triggers (what reminds them to listen)
   1. Context (how, where, when, how often)
   1. Content (show types, lengths, appeal, management [for the purpose of finding new shows])
2. How do they discover new shows?
3. How do they decide to continue listening to a new show?
4. How might they see themselves using the concepts presented to them?

## Methodology
Virtual 1hr long semi-structured interviews with 4-6 North American based participants with a mix of demographics.

## Recruitment criteria
Aim to have a mix of gender, age, and ethnicities
1. (Established behavior) Listens to 1-2 podcasts/week

- Age ranges: 18-24; 25-39; 40-60; 61+
- Income ranges: <25K; 25-49K; 50-74K; 75-99K; 100-150K; 150K+

## Moderator guide

### Intro
- At any point they can stop the interview
- Get verbal consent to record
- Confirmed they've signed the consent form
- TLDR on the project and how their data will be used

### Warm-up (softball questions to get them warmed up)
1. Any new favoriate episodes? 

### Current behavior
1. How often would you say you listen to a podcast?
2. What kind of shows appeal to you? Why?
3. What does a typical day look like when you're listening to a show? Where are you? How do you listen (e.g. in car, youtube)?
   (If participant doesn't naturally bring up the following, inquire about:)
   1.  How do you know which show you'll be listening to? (e.g. queued?)
   2.  Are you doing anything else while you're listening? Are you listening with others?
   3.  What do you do when a podcast ends?
4. Do you look for new shows? How do you discover them?
   1. (i.e. do you look under a category?) 
   2. When you start listening to a new show, what do you look for before deciding to give it a chance?

### Concept testing
1. We'd like to walk through a few concepts with you and get your feedback on them. These are rough, early ideas, nothing is precious. 
2. What would you call this idea in your own words?
3. Do you have any questions about it?
4. What do you think out it? What stands out to you?
   1. What do like most? Least?
5. Does it remind you of other products you've seen or used? Why?
6. Who do you think it's designed for?
7. Is there anything confusing or unclear about this? What could we do to make it more understandable?
   1. Anything you'd want explained before using it?
   2. Are there parts you feel unsure about? Why?
8. Do you think you would use this? Why or why not?
   1. When and how might you use this? In what situations would it be most helpful?
   2. How does this compare to the way you're currently listening to podcasts?
      1. How might it fit into your existing routines/habits?
   3. Do you think this concept would make your discovery of new podcasts faster? Easier? More enjoyable?
9. How would you expect to learn about this product and how to use it?
10. Thumbs up? Thumbs down?
    1.  How do you feel about the idea? 
    2.  Would you recommend we move forward with it? 
    3.  Are there any changes you'd recommend?

### Outro 
1. Anything else you'd like to tell us?
