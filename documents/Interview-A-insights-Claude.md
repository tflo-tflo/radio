# Podcast Discovery Research Insights

## Research Question 1: Current Podcast Listening Behaviors

### Triggers (what reminds them to listen)
- **Queue-based listening**: Participant has podcasts queued up and lets the next episode auto-play
- **No specific triggers mentioned**: Listening seems habitual rather than event-driven
- **Phase-based engagement**: Goes through phases with different podcast types (historical, crime, etc.)

### Context (how, where, when, how often)
- **Morning routine**: Listens while getting ready in the morning
- **Continuous listening**: Uses "Up Next" feature to keep content flowing without manual selection
- **High volume**: Follows many different podcasts across various phases
- **All-day listening**: Mentioned listening "all morning" to different shows

### Content (show types, lengths, appeal, management)

#### Show Types
- **Historical podcasts** (goes through phases)
- **Crime podcasts** (used to listen more)
- **Tech podcasts** (Hardcore, Search Engine, Better Offline)
- **Comedy** (follows comedian Josh Gondelman)
- **Cambodian language podcasts** (to hear Cambodian spoken, news about Cambodia)
- **Local news podcasts**
- **Serialized investigative shows** (pyramid scheme podcast)

#### Appeal Factors
1. **Learning something new** - explicitly stated as important
2. **Host qualities**: Knowledgeable AND/OR funny
3. **Interesting topics** that change week to week
4. **Format-topic fit**: Format needs to suit the content
5. **Minimal ads**
6. **Cultural connection**: Seeks Cambodian-language content for language exposure

#### Management for Finding New Shows
- **Network effect is primary**: Follows hosts to their new projects
- **Guest appearances**: Discovers new shows when favorite hosts/comedians guest on them
  - Behind the Bastards → Weird Little Guy
  - Behind the Bastards → Better Offline
- **Searches for specific people**: Types comedian/host names to find all their appearances
- **Category browsing**: Sometimes finds shows in same category clusters (tech, historical)
- **Minimal use of platform recommendations**: Apple Podcasts homepage rarely yields shows they like

## Research Question 2: How Do They Discover New Shows?

### Primary Discovery Method: Social/Network Chain
1. **Friend recommendations** (mentioned first)
2. **Host migration**: Following hosts to their new shows
   - "Hyperfix" - host from another show they listened to
   - "Interesting Things Explained Well" - host of a host
   - "Search Engine" - host went off to do his own thing
3. **Guest appearances**: Key discovery mechanism
   - Listens to shows because a favorite person guested on it
   - Example: Josh Gondelman appearances led to trying new shows
4. **Cross-pollination**: "Connection of a connection" - discovering through networks

### Secondary Discovery Methods
- **Category/sector suggestions**: Tech podcasts suggest other tech podcasts
- **Platform homepage**: Tried but doesn't usually like what they find there
- **Local content**: Actively seeks local news podcasts
- **Specific topic searches**: Mentioned difficulty finding "linguist podcasts"

### Discovery Chain Pattern
> "It's just like, like a chain" - describes their discovery process as interconnected rather than platform-driven

## Research Question 3: How Do They Decide to Continue Listening?

### Trial Period
- **1-2 episodes** to evaluate
- Sometimes searches for specific episodes or guest appearances to sample

### Decision Criteria (in rough order of importance)

#### Must-Haves
1. **Learning potential**: "If I learn something"
2. **Interesting topic** that changes week to week
3. **Host qualities**: Knowledgeable or funny (or both)

#### Format Considerations
4. **Format-topic fit**: The format must suit the subject matter
   - Example: Serialized format worked for pyramid scheme deep-dives
   - Same host switching to episodic format for different topics weekly didn't work
5. **Ad load**: Mentions "whether or not there's like a lot of ads"

#### Deal-Breakers
- **Format mismatch**: When serialized show became episodic, participant stopped listening
  - "It wasn't as interesting... I don't think it fit as well"
- **Heavy ads**
- **Topic doesn't change**: Wants variety week to week

## Research Question 4: How Might They Use the Concepts Presented?

### Positive Reception

#### CONCEPT 2: Current Events in Your Area ⭐ STRONGEST INTEREST
**Why it resonated:**
- Addresses a real pain point: "When I'm trying to learn about something, having a better way to group... was quite hard to find"
- Example: Struggled to find linguist podcasts
- Serves two needs:
  1. "Learn about stuff I don't know that's happening" (discovery)
  2. "I know about the stuff happening. I want to learn more" (deep-dive)
- Art Walk example resonated: "Would be cool to be like, oh, these things are happening around me"

**Concerns/Questions:**
- Wants ability to dive deeper: "If this is interesting, is there more?"
- Wants customization: Which events/topics appear first
- Distinction between "breaking news vs what's important to me"

#### CONCEPT 3: Format-Based (Serialized vs. Episodic) - CONDITIONAL INTEREST
**Why it could work:**
- "There are times when I listen to a serialized one, I'm like, I would love to listen to another one that's similar"
- Would be helpful for finding "more of similar ones I knew I liked already"

**Why they're unsure:**
- "I don't know that until I already listen to it and like it"
- Doesn't actively seek format first: "It's more like, oh, I find this podcast and then..."
- Not a search criterion but could be useful for recommendations post-discovery

#### CONCEPT 6: Cross-Referencing Podcasts - HELPFUL
- "I think that would be helpful"
- Already does this manually (searches for hosts/comedians, finds their appearances)
- Would automate existing behavior
- Most useful for podcasts already listening to

#### CONCEPT 4: Breaking News vs. Evergreen Journalism - HELPFUL IF COMBINED
- "Some mixture of all of these is helpful"
- Wants to see:
  - Stuff close to them that's breaking news
  - Episodic ones that are in-depth journalism
  - Serialized ones that are in-depth journalism
- Suggests need for multi-dimensional filtering

### Neutral/Unclear Reception

#### CONCEPT 1: Tone-Based Grouping - NOT HOW THEY THINK
- "I don't know if that's how I filter"
- "I've never done it"
- Example: "I'm trying to think... would I seek out like a really funny historical podcast? I don't know if that's how I filter"
- Has hosts they like but "I don't know if that's the reason I seek out podcasts"

#### CONCEPT 5: Conversational vs. Structured Style - NOT HOW THEY THINK
- "I don't think I ever really think that way"
- Brief, dismissive response suggests low relevance

#### CONCEPT 8: Time of Day - NOT HOW THEY THINK
- "I don't really think of it that way either"
- "Listen to different ones all morning"
- No differentiation in their current behavior

#### CONCEPT 7: Opposing Viewpoints - DEPENDS ON EXECUTION
**Interested IF:**
- "News that offers impartial perspective" (journalism-focused)
- Not talk shows, but balanced reporting

**Not interested IF:**
- "Far right negative stuff" - doesn't want extreme opposing views
- Even if good to have diverse views, wouldn't personally listen
- Concerns about impartiality

### Physical Product Concepts

#### CONCEPT 9: Physical Radio Device - SOME INTEREST BUT NEEDS REFINEMENT

**Who it's for (participant's perception):**
- "Someone who doesn't want to be on their phone"
- "Want something more tactile, on their desk"
- Ability to set topics they know they like, let episodes change

**How they'd use it:**
- Similar to current "Up Next" auto-play behavior
- "Let the next thing play" - expects automatic progression through episodes
- "More designed version" of their current queue behavior

**Critical feedback:**
1. **Needs more depth**: "Is there more?" - wants ability to explore beyond curated episodes
2. **Needs customization**: 
   - "My first impression would be... these are things important to me, I could change these"
   - Wants to prioritize "comedy" or other preferences
   - "Breaking news vs what's important to me"
   - Should customize via separate app, not on device
3. **Needs episode details**: 
   - Currently checks episode descriptions to remember details ("this is the restaurant they talked about")
   - Listens in morning while getting ready - needs reference info
   - Wants to click and see descriptions

**Form factor preference:**
- Desk version preferred over handheld
- Handheld version got "thumbs down"

#### CONCEPT 10: Handheld Radio - THUMBS DOWN

**Why it doesn't work:**
- **No preview**: "You don't really know what's playing"
- **Ads play first**: "It's just like an ad is the first thing"
- **Can't see next episode**: Unlike the desk version where "you could see actually what the next episode is"

**Suggested fix:**
- "Turning the dial switched it and you could see here what switched"
- "Turn the dial, it showed me what it was, I could click the dial and say yeah, I want to play that one"
- Preview first, then commit to play

#### CONCEPT 11: App Version - DOESN'T DIFFERENTIATE ENOUGH

**Core problem:**
- "It doesn't really show me anything new. It looks like Apple"
- "I know exactly how it works"
- "The new part is just the categories"
- **"It doesn't really feel any different to me"**

**Barrier to adoption:**
- "You have to move all the rest of my podcasts into a new podcast app"
- Content differentiation isn't enough
- "The episodes aren't presented in a different way"
- Needs different **experience**, not just different content organization

**Key insight:**
> "The discovery is not presented in like a new format that like looks like a radio versus what Apple is showing. And so it doesn't feel like something different."

- Even if content/categorization is different, experience must be meaningfully different
- Visual and interaction design matter as much as content strategy

## Key Themes & Patterns

### 1. Network-Driven Discovery Over Platform Recommendations
- Discovery is fundamentally **social and relationship-based**
- Follows people (hosts, guests, comedians) not just topics
- Platform recommendations rarely work for them
- "Chain" metaphor: each discovery leads to next through human connections

### 2. Post-Discovery Learning is Important
- Actively checks episode descriptions during/after listening
- Wants to remember details (restaurant names, etc.)
- Information retrieval is part of the listening experience

### 3. Context-Specific Needs vs. Habitual Listening
- **Habitual**: Auto-play queue, morning routine, phases with different genres
- **Contextual**: Sometimes wants to learn about specific topics/events
- Current tools serve habitual better than contextual

### 4. Format Follows Function
- Format must match content type
- Serialized works for deep-dive topics
- Episodic works for variety
- Mismatch causes abandonment

### 5. Multi-Dimensional Filtering Needed
- Wants combinations: "Serialized + in-depth journalism + about events near me"
- Single-dimension filters feel incomplete
- "Mixture of all of these" approach preferred

### 6. Experience Differentiation > Content Differentiation
- Won't switch apps just for different categorization
- Needs meaningfully different interaction model
- Physical device offers this; standard app doesn't
- Barrier to switching is high without experience innovation

## Recommendations

### Highest Priority
1. **Build location + event-based discovery** (Concept 2)
   - Allow both curation AND search/exploration
   - Make it customizable (user sets priority events/topics)
   - Enable deep-dive beyond curated episodes

2. **Enable cross-referencing/guest tracking** (Concept 6)
   - Automate what power users already do manually
   - "More shows like this" based on host/guest networks
   - Show guest appearances of favorite people

### Medium Priority
3. **Multi-dimensional filtering**
   - Combine format + content type + timeliness
   - Example: "Serialized in-depth journalism about local events"

4. **If building physical device:**
   - Focus on desk/stationary version
   - Must allow customization via companion app
   - Must show episode details/descriptions
   - Must preview before auto-playing next episode
   - Design for "set it and let it flow" behavior

### Low Priority
5. **Single-dimension filters:**
   - Tone-based grouping
   - Time-of-day suggestions
   - Conversational vs. structured style
   - These don't match mental models for discovery

### Avoid
6. **Standard app with just new categories**
   - Insufficient differentiation from existing apps
   - High switching barrier
   - Must pair content innovation with experience innovation
